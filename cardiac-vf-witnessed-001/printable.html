Defibrillation was delayed beyond 90 seconds after VF was identified, often because the crew prioritized IV access, advanced airway placement, or extra CPR cycles before the first shock. Branch - Re-Arrest from Hyperventilation / Untreated Hypotension
Trigger: Hyperventilation, untreated hypotension, or pad removal in the post-ROSC period
Despite ROSC, the team either hyperventilated the patient (RR > 12), failed to address falling SBP, or removed the defibrillator pads. Within minutes the patient re-arrests - frequently back to VF. This branch teaches that the post-ROSC period is the highest risk for re-arrest and that pads must remain on.

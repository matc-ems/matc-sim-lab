Branch - Delayed Defibrillation - Continued VF
Defibrillation delayed > 90 seconds after VF identified, or compressions interrupted excessively for procedures
Defibrillation was delayed beyond 90 seconds after VF was identified, often because the crew prioritized IV access, advanced airway placement, or extra CPR cycles before the first shock. The patient remains in coarse VF deteriorating toward fine VF. Each minute of delayed defibrillation in VF reduces survival by approximately 7-10%. The team must recognize the priority error, defibrillate immediately, and add antiarrhythmic therapy if VF is shock-refractory.

Branch - Re-Arrest from Hyperventilation / Untreated Hypotension
Hyperventilation, untreated hypotension, or pad removal in the post-ROSC period
Despite ROSC, the team either hyperventilated the patient (RR > 12), failed to address falling SBP, or removed the defibrillator pads. Within minutes the patient re-arrests - frequently back to VF. The team must immediately reapply pads (or recognize they were never removed), restart compressions, and re-shock. This branch teaches that the post-ROSC period is the highest risk for re-arrest and that pads must remain on.

Learning Objectives
  • Recognize witnessed cardiac arrest with bystander CPR as a survivable event when high-quality CPR and rapid defibrillation are delivered
  • Apply the cardiac arrest pit-crew model with defined roles and a code leader
  • Defibrillate witnessed VF as soon as the device is ready - no required delay for additional CPR cycles when bystander CPR has been adequate
  • Use the manufacturer's recommended (or highest) energy setting for biphasic defibrillation (200 J on Zoll X-Series)
  • Resume compressions immediately after defibrillation without a pulse check for 2 minutes
  • Recognize ROSC by multiple methods: organized rhythm, palpable pulse, EtCO2 surge
  • Apply post-ROSC priorities: airway, ventilation 8-10/min, SpO2 94-98%, BP support, 12-lead, destination decision
  • Identify Inferior STEMI on 12-lead ECG (II, III, aVF with reciprocal changes in I, aVL) and consider RV involvement
  • Activate the regional STEMI system from the field and transmit the 12-lead to the receiving facility
  • Avoid the two leading causes of post-ROSC re-arrest: hyperventilation and untreated hypotension

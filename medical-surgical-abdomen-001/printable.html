Expected Actions
critical
Primary survey; recognize shock (HR 124, BP 86/58, EtCO2 30)
Inclusion Criteria: Signs of poor perfusion (due to a medical cause) such as one or more of the following: Altered mental status; Delayed capillary refill (> 3 seconds); Decreased urine output; Respiratory rate greater than 20 breaths per minute in adults; Hypotension for age; Tachycardia; Weak, decreased or bounding pulses; Cool/mottled or flushed/ruddy skin.
↳ MATC: Shock
critical
Assess for perforated bowel and AAA (palpate, femoral pulses)
Assess for life-threatening causes of abdominal pain, which may include: Signs and symptoms of ischemic, necrotic, or perforated bowel: Severe tenderness; Abdominal pain with motion or palpation of the abdomen; Fever; Bloody stool; Nausea and vomiting; Absence of passage of stool or gas; Abdominal distention, with tympany to percussion. Signs and symptoms of dissecting or ruptured abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA): Unequal femoral or distal lower extremity pulses; 'Pulsatile' abdominal mass; Associated back pain and/or chest pain; Known history of abdominal aortic aneurysm.

Improper Care Branch
Bradycardia not treated; PEA arrest
Trigger: Symptomatic 3rd degree AV block was not recognized or treated; patient deteriorated from symptomatic bradycardia to PEA arrest during transport.
Crew obtained a monitor strip but did not recognize the 3rd degree AV block, did not place pacer pads, and proceeded with passive supportive transport with the patient on a nasal cannula and IV access only. En route the heart rate slowed further to 22 with a junctional escape, and shortly thereafter the patient lost pulses with continued slow electrical activity on the monitor — pulseless electrical activity arrest. Crew must now recognize the deterioration, begin high-quality CPR, manage the arrest, and consider the bradyarrhythmic etiology.

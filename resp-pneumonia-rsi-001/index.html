CRITICAL Immediately remove CPAP mask and perform aggressive suctioning with Yankauer, consider SALAD (Suction Assisted Laryngoscopy Airway Decontamination) technique.

CRITICAL Give Normal Saline 1 L IV/IO wide open — consider reversible causes (H's and T's). Hypovolemia (sepsis/dehydration) and Hypoxia are the top causes here.
CRITICAL Once airway is adequately suctioned, place ETT or SGA to protect the airway and deliver effective ventilation. Confirm with waveform capnography.
CRITICAL Address reversible causes (H's & T's): Hypoxia (airway/ventilation), Hypovolemia (fluids), Hypo/hyperkalemia, Hydrogen ion (acidosis — bicarbonate controversial), Hypothermia, Tension pneumothorax, Tamponade, Toxins, Thrombosis (PE/coronary).
CRITICAL Provide code notification to receiving hospital; transport emergently with ongoing CPR.
Learning Objectives
  • Recognize septic shock from SIRS criteria + hypotension + infectious source
  • Apply MATC Shock protocol with attention to fluid resuscitation strategy in a patient with known HFrEF
  • Identify absolute and relative contraindications to CPAP/BiPAP (depressed LOC, hemodynamic instability, vomiting risk)
  • Recognize NIV failure early: declining mental status, rising EtCO2, poor mask tolerance, no improvement in SpO2
  • Execute a structured RSI sequence with protocol-appropriate medication doses and clear team roles
  • Select induction agents thoughtfully in septic patients (ketamine preferred over etomidate; both protocol-permitted)
  • Anticipate post-intubation hypotension (the 'HOP killers': Hypotension, Hypoxia, pH issues) and treat proactively
  • Apply MATC Shock vasopressor guidance: Norepinephrine as first-line in distributive/septic shock

Initial Contact
EMS arrives to find a 68-year-old female on the bathroom floor, pale, diaphoretic, and weak. Initial monitor connection shows a slow regular rhythm in the high 30s. The teaching point sets up: this is symptomatic bradycardia with high-grade AV block on a patient who is on a beta-blocker AND is bradycardic enough to be near-syncopal. Atropine may have limited effect because of the high block AND the chronic beta-blockade. Crew should anticipate pacing — and a key teaching beat is to plan sedation/analgesia using fentanyl (per Pain Management) BEFORE pacing rather than after the patient is in obvious distress.

Trigger: TCP started without pre-pacing analgesia; alert patient experiencing significant pain and agitation; reactive analgesia required.
Crew jumped straight to pacing without pre-medicating with fentanyl. Within seconds the patient is in significant pain from the pacing-induced muscle contractions and is fighting/moving on the cot, which makes capture confirmation difficult and adds anxiety.

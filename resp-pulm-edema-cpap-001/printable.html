The clinical picture (JVD, bilateral rales to mid-lung fields, pink frothy sputum, peripheral edema, orthopnea history) points strongly to acute decompensated heart failure. The crew must recognize acute pulmonary edema, begin high-flow oxygen, obtain a 12-lead to screen for STEMI, establish IV access, screen for PDE5 inhibitors, and prepare for NTG + CPAP.

Improper Care Branch
Cardioversion without sedation
Trigger: Synchronized cardioversion delivered without pre-shock sedation/analgesia in an alert patient; rhythm converted but patient suffered significant pain and psychological distress.
Crew cardioverted without giving any procedural sedation or analgesia. The shock was effective and the rhythm converted to sinus, but the alert patient experienced the full pain of the shock. He yells, becomes anxious and tearful, and the experience leaves a lasting psychological imprint that may affect his willingness to seek care for future episodes. The protocol explicitly says 'For responsive patients, consider sedation and analgesia' — this is the moment to do it, not after.

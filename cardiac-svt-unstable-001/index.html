Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate stable from unstable tachycardia using the unstable criteria: hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain, signs of shock, acute heart failure
  • Apply the SVT algorithm: vagal -> adenosine 6 mg -> adenosine 12 mg -> if stable, calcium channel blocker or beta-blocker; if unstable, synchronized cardioversion
  • Use the modified Valsalva maneuver (15 sec forced expiration against syringe + supine with passive leg raise) for higher conversion rates
  • Administer adenosine via the most proximal IV possible with rapid 20 mL flush due to its 10-second half-life
  • Verify SYNCHRONIZED mode before every cardioversion and re-engage SYNC after each shock if escalating
  • Use 100 J biphasic as the typical first-shock energy for unstable SVT (escalate to 150, 200 J as needed)
  • Provide procedural sedation (midazolam) and analgesia (fentanyl) for any conscious patient receiving synchronized cardioversion
  • Identify post-conversion ECG findings: pre-excitation (delta waves) implies WPW and changes future therapy
  • Recognize iatrogenic VF from asynchronous shock as a preventable error of missed SYNC verification

CRITICAL Once obstruction is removed, provide BVM ventilation at 15:2 ratio during CPR. If advanced airway placed (SGA preferred over ETI in pediatrics), ventilate 1 breath every 3-5 seconds (12-20/min) without interrupting compressions.
IMPORTANT Consider and address reversible causes (Hs and Ts): hypoxia (primary — obstruction), hypovolemia, hypoglycemia, hypothermia, hyperkalemia, tension pneumothorax, tamponade, toxins, thrombosis.
SUPPLEMENTAL Debrief the delay immediately post-call: once a choking patient becomes unresponsive, back blows/abdominal thrusts are REPLACED by CPR — continuing them delays definitive care and leads to arrest.
Learning Objectives
  • Apply the AHA FBAO algorithm distinction between responsive (back blows/abdominal thrusts) and unresponsive (CPR with airway inspection at each breath) patients
  • Explain why chest compressions replace back blows once an FBAO patient becomes unresponsive
  • Demonstrate direct laryngoscopy and pediatric Magill forceps technique for a visualized foreign body
  • State and defend the absolute prohibition on blind finger sweeps
  • Apply pediatric CPR ratios: 15:2 two-rescuer, 30:2 single-rescuer, with depth one-third AP chest diameter and rate 100-120/min
  • Justify the prehospital preference for BVM/SGA over endotracheal intubation in pediatric patients
  • Apply post-ROSC care principles: SpO2 94-98%, EtCO2 35-40 mmHg, avoid hyperoxia and hyperventilation, serial 12-leads, glucose check, pediatric-capable destination

ST depression V1–V3 + tall R waves
Trigger: Standard 12-lead acquired showing ST depression V1–V3 with tall R waves and upright T waves — pattern suspicious for posterior STEMI; posterior leads needed for confirmation.
Standard 12-lead shows ST depression of 2–3 mm in V1, V2, V3 with R waves > S waves in V1 and V2 and upright T waves in those leads. There is NO ST elevation on the standard 12-lead. The reciprocal pattern (anterior depression + tall R wave) IS the posterior STEMI footprint. The decision now is whether to acquire posterior leads (V7, V8, V9) — if any show ≥ 0.5 mm ST elevation, posterior STEMI is confirmed. Crew should also consider this could represent acute posterior MI from circumflex or RCA occlusion.

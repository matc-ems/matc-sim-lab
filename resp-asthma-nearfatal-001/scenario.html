Post-Intubation Ventilation Strategy
Successful RSI with tube placement
Tube confirmed. The hard part is ventilating — high airway pressures, severe air trapping, and risk of pneumothorax. Low rate, long exhalation, small tidal volumes, and permissive hypercapnia are the rules. If the patient decompensates suddenly after intubation, disconnect the circuit and let them exhale.

Learning Objectives
  • Recognize near-fatal asthma and apply MATC Respiratory Distress protocol aggressively
  • Use CPAP with in-line bronchodilators to avoid intubation
  • Understand magnesium sulfate as a rescue therapy in severe bronchoconstriction
  • Apply a modified ventilation strategy to avoid breath stacking in asthma
  • Use ketamine as the induction agent of choice in status asthmaticus

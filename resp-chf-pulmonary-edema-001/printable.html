important
Set up IV nitroglycerin infusion titrated to BP (effective preload + afterload reduction)
Nitroglycerin 0.8 mg SL, can repeat every 5 minutes for SBP greater than 100 mmHg. Consider IV nitroglycerin infusion titrated to blood pressure. Notes: Nitroglycerin reduces left ventricular filling pressure primarily via venous dilation. At higher doses the drug variably lowers systemic afterload and increases stroke volume and cardiac output. Nitrates provide both subjective and objective improvement, and might decrease intubation rates, incidence of MIs, and mortality. High-dose nitrates can reduce both preload and afterload and potentially increase cardiac output and blood pressure.
↳ MATC: Respiratory Distress (includes Bronchospasm, Pulmonary Edema)
critical
Do NOT push prehospital furosemide — pulmonary edema is volume distribution, not total volume overload
Pulmonary edema is more commonly a problem of volume distribution than total body fluid overload, so administration of diuretics such as furosemide provide no immediate benefit for most patients and can cause significant harm. Inducement of inappropriate diuresis can lead to increased morbidity and mortality in patients with other disease processes such as pneumonia and sepsis.

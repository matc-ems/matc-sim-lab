Expected Actions
CRITICAL Approach calmly, introduce yourself at the child's eye level, and allow the child to remain in the mother's lap in her position of comfort. Do NOT separate the child from the parent. Minimize the number of providers directly interacting with the child.
CRITICAL Perform focused assessment: listen for stridor at rest versus only with agitation, observe work of breathing (retractions, nasal flaring, accessory muscle use), assess mental status, skin color, and air entry. Obtain full vital signs including temperature and SpO2.
IMPORTANT Apply continuous pulse oximetry and waveform capnography (nasal cannula sampling line or mask-integrated) as tolerated without agitating the child. Attach cardiac monitor with minimal handling.
IMPORTANT Administer blow-by oxygen via simple O2 tubing or parent-held pediatric mask at 6-10 L/min near the child's face. Titrate escalation (NC → simple mask → NRB) to SpO2 94-98%. Do NOT force a mask if it worsens agitation.
IMPORTANT Rule out mimics before committing to the croup pathway: (1) No drooling or 'hot potato' voice or tripoding (argues against epiglottitis); (2) No sudden choking event or witnessed aspiration (argues against foreign body); (3) No hives, lip/tongue swelling, or allergen exposure (argues against anaphylaxis); (4) Age >2 and no wheezing (argues against bronchiolitis); (5) Vaccinations up to date including Hib.
SUPPLEMENTAL Obtain SAMPLE/OPQRST from parents without hovering over the child: onset, fever history, sick contacts, prior croup episodes, choking history, vaccinations, allergies, and home treatments already tried.
SUPPLEMENTAL Coach the parents: explain that keeping the child calm is part of treatment, that the barky cough and stridor are classic for croup, and that medications given tonight typically work quickly.

IMPORTANT Expedite transport to pediatric-capable ED; consider priority return if decompensation continues despite interventions. Early notification to the receiving facility.
SUPPLEMENTAL Re-engage parents calmly: acknowledge the difficulty, explain what happened and what you are doing now, and recruit the parent to hold the nebulizer mask and soothe the child.
Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate croup from epiglottitis, foreign body aspiration, anaphylaxis, and bronchiolitis using history and exam
  • Recognize stridor AT REST as the marker of severe croup requiring inhaled epinephrine
  • Apply the calm-approach principle: agitation narrows the airway — keeping the child in the parent's lap is a clinical intervention, not a comfort measure
  • Dose nebulized epinephrine correctly: Racemic epinephrine 2.25% 0.5 mL in 2.5 mL NS OR Epinephrine 1 mg/mL 5 mg nebulized; may repeat q20 min
  • Dose dexamethasone correctly: 0.6 mg/kg PO/IV/IM to a max of 16 mg; PO or IM preferred to avoid unnecessary IV access
  • Understand that a single dose of dexamethasone is sufficient per protocol
  • Explain why humidified oxygen / mist therapy is NOT indicated per the MATC croup protocol
  • State the mandatory-transport rule: every child who receives inhaled epinephrine must be transported for monitoring because of rebound risk
  • Recognize the ominous signs of impending respiratory failure (mental status change, pallor, dusky color, decreased retractions with decreasing stridor and breath sounds)

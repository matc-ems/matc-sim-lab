Expected Actions
critical
Approach calmly, introduce yourself at the child's eye level, and allow the child to remain in the mother's lap in her position of comfort. Do NOT separate the child from the parent. Minimize the number of providers directly interacting with the child.
Agitation and crying increase airway turbulence and worsen stridor in croup. A calm approach is the single most important non-pharmacologic intervention.
↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
critical
Perform focused assessment: listen for stridor at rest versus only with agitation, observe work of breathing (retractions, nasal flaring, accessory muscle use), assess mental status, skin color, and air entry. Obtain full vital signs including temperature and SpO2.
Stridor at rest identifies severe croup requiring inhaled epinephrine. Mental status change, pallor, dusky appearance, or decreasing stridor with decreased breath sounds indicates impending respiratory failure.
↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
important
Apply continuous pulse oximetry and waveform capnography (nasal cannula sampling line or mask-integrated) as tolerated without agitating the child. Attach cardiac monitor with minimal handling.
Pulse oximetry and EtCO2 are routine adjuncts to respiratory monitoring per croup protocol.
↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
important
Administer blow-by oxygen via simple O2 tubing or parent-held pediatric mask at 6-10 L/min near the child's face. Titrate escalation (NC → simple mask → NRB) to SpO2 94-98%. Do NOT force a mask if it worsens agitation.
Croup protocol directs escalation of supplemental oxygen to achieve SpO2 94-98% while avoiding agitation that worsens upper airway obstruction.
↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
important
Rule out mimics before committing to the croup pathway: (1) No drooling or 'hot potato' voice or tripoding (argues against epiglottitis); (2) No sudden choking event or witnessed aspiration (argues against foreign body); (3) No hives, lip/tongue swelling, or allergen exposure (argues against anaphylaxis); (4) Age >2 and no wheezing (argues against bronchiolitis); (5) Vaccinations up to date including Hib.
The croup protocol explicitly excludes anaphylaxis, asthma, bronchiolitis, foreign body aspiration, submersion, and epiglottitis. Ruling these out protects the patient from a wrong-pathway error.
↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
supplemental
Obtain SAMPLE/OPQRST from parents without hovering over the child: onset, fever history, sick contacts, prior croup episodes, choking history, vaccinations, allergies, and home treatments already tried.
History directs differential and confirms croup pathway.

supplemental
Coach the parents: explain that keeping the child calm is part of treatment, that the barky cough and stridor are classic for croup, and that medications given tonight typically work quickly.
Parent anxiety transmits to the child. A calm parent = a calm patient = a more open airway.

critical

↳ MATC: Pediatric Respiratory Distress (Croup)
critical
Prepare nebulized epinephrine. Preferred: Racemic epinephrine 2.25% solution, 0.5 mL mixed with 2.5 mL normal saline, via pediatric nebulizer at 6-8 L/min O2.

Expected Actions
critical
Acquire 12-lead EKG within 10 minutes of patient contact
The 12-lead EKG is the primary diagnostic tool that identifies a STEMI; it is imperative that EMS clinicians routinely acquire a 12-lead EKG within 10 minutes for all patients exhibiting signs and symptoms of ACS. Performance of serial EKGs is encouraged for symptomatic patients with EKGs initially non-diagnostic for STEMI.
↳ MATC: Chest Pain/Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)/ST-segment Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)
critical
Read 12-lead for high-risk features (prolonged QT, Brugada, delta waves)
Consider high-risk 12-lead EKG features including, but not limited to: Evidence of QT prolongation (generally over 500 msec); Delta waves; Brugada syndrome (incomplete right bundle branch block (RBBB) pattern in V1/V2 with ST segment elevation); Hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy.

Expected Actions
critical
Immediate high-quality chest compressions; pull over to resuscitate on scene if possible
The most important therapies for patients suffering from cardiac arrest are prompt cardiac defibrillation for shockable rhythms and minimally interrupted effective chest compressions. Patient Safety Considerations: Performing manual chest compressions in a moving vehicle may pose a clinician safety concern. Ideally, patients should be resuscitated as close to the scene as operationally possible.
↳ MATC: Cardiac Arrest (VF/VT/Asystole/PEA)
critical
Apply pads; defibrillate at 200 J (Zoll biphasic) for shockable rhythm
Defibrillation dosing should follow manufacturer's recommendation in the case of biphasic defibrillators. If the manufacturer's recommendation is unknown, use highest setting possible. Zoll: Adult - 120 J, 150 J, 200 J.
↳ MATC: Cardiac Arrest (VF/VT/Asystole/PEA)
critical
Establish IV/IO; Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO during first or second cycle
If feasible, IV or IO access should be obtained. Administer Epinephrine 1mg during the first or second round of compressions. Prioritize early administration of epinephrine for non-shockable rhythms.
↳ MATC: Cardiac Arrest (VF/VT/Asystole/PEA)
important
Magnesium sulfate 2 g IV over 1-2 minutes for torsades / polymorphic VT
For torsades de pointes, give magnesium sulfate 2 g IV administered over 1-2 minutes (or 25-50 mg/kg for pediatrics). There is insufficient evidence to recommend for or against the routine administration during cardiac arrest.

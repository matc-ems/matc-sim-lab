↳ MATC: Hypoglycemia
critical
Recognize falling RR with rising HR as decompensation — prepare for BVM and pre-notify ED
In most adults, tachycardia is the first sign of compensated shock, and may persist for hours. Tachycardia can be a late sign of shock in children and a tachycardic child may be close to cardiovascular collapse. Hypotension indicates uncompensated shock, which may progress to cardiopulmonary failure within minutes. Hypotension is a late and ominous sign in pediatric uncompensated shock.

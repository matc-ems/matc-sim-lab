Improper Care Branch
Electrical capture, no perfusion check
Trigger: Crew started pacing, observed pacer spikes followed by wide QRS on the monitor, declared 'electrical capture' and stopped titrating output. They did not palpate a synchronized femoral or carotid pulse. Despite the monitor showing 'capture,' the patient remains hypotensive (BP 76/40) and AMS — because the apparent QRS on the monitor is an artifact at sub-threshold output, not true myocardial depolarization. This is false electrical capture: the most dangerous pacing failure mode because the monitor reassures the crew while the patient continues to die.
Crew saw a pacer spike followed by a wide QRS complex on every beat, called 'we have capture!' and stopped escalating output. What they did not do was palpate a femoral or carotid pulse synchronized with each pacer spike. The reality: at the output they stopped at, the pacer is producing an electrical artifact on the monitor that LOOKS like a captured QRS but is below the threshold for mechanical ventricular contraction — classic false capture. The patient's actual cardiac output is unchanged from the pre-pacing complete heart block state. BP remains 76/40, mental status is unchanged (A&Ox2 → still drowsy, intermittently responsive), and skin remains cool and diaphoretic. The monitor is lying to them and they have to learn to mistrust it. The recovery: palpate the femoral pulse RIGHT NOW, find it is NOT synchronized with the pacer spikes, increase mA stepwise until a real synchronized femoral pulse appears, then reconfirm BP and mental status improve.

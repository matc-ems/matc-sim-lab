Initial Contact
EMS arrives to a 64-year-old man in late septic shock from pneumonia with frank respiratory failure: GCS 7, RR 30 and shallow, SpO2 84% on a two-person BVM with a 5 cmH2O PEEP valve and 15 L/min O2, EtCO2 62 with a shark-fin overlay, BP 88/52, HR 128 sinus tach, mottled and febrile. He needs an advanced airway — but he carries every classic difficult-airway predictor: BMI 31, short thick neck, Mallampati 4, a full beard (poor mask seal), and a receding jaw. The teaching beat for Tier 3 is that Plan B and Plan C must exist BEFORE Plan A is started: identify the difficult airway, optimize BVM/PEEP and pre-oxygenation, set a hard attempt limit (2 attempts then SGA), have a bougie and an SGA prepped and accessible, and verbalize the CICO bail-out before the first attempt. The default arc is: pre-intubation optimization → first laryngoscopy attempt fails (cannot visualize cords) → restore oxygenation between attempts → second attempt succeeds with bougie/video/positioning adjustments. Two judgment-error branches: (1) tube fixation — repeated attempts without restoring oxygenation, sats crash to 70 then 60, peri-arrest; recovery requires stopping, oxygenating, placing an SGA. (2) Missed esophageal intubation — first attempt feels right, but EtCO2 is flat, no chest rise, gastric sounds; if the student misses confirmation and continues to ventilate, vomiting + further desat → pull, oxygenate, reinsert.

↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical
Two-person, two-thumbs-up BVM with PEEP 5 cmH2O, head elevated 30 degrees, OPA in place
Use bag-valve-mask (BVM) ventilation in the setting of respiratory failure or arrest. Whenever possible, the patient's head should be elevated up to 30 degrees. Two-person, two-thumbs-up BVM ventilation is preferred. PEEP should be used with BVM — 5 cmH2O is generally an appropriate initial PEEP setting. Oropharyngeal airways (OPA) or nasopharyngeal airways (NPA) can be placed if needed to maintain a patent airway and make BVM ventilation more effective.
↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical
Pre-oxygenate ≥2 min with BVM/PEEP; place high-flow NC at 15 L/min for apneic oxygenation
Patient should be pre-oxygenated with high flow oxygen, and positive pressure ventilation for a minimum of two minutes prior to the procedure. In some circumstances, sedation may be required for adequate pre-oxygenation. In addition to preoxygenation, apneic oxygenation (high-flow oxygen by nasal cannula) may prolong the period before hypoxia during an intubation attempt.
↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical

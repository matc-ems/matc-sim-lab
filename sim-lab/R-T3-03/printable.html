↳ MATC: Anaphylaxis and Allergic Reaction
Improper Care Branch
Insufficient fluid resus
Trigger: Crew gives IM epi repeats and starts an epi infusion but limits NS to a single 250 mL bolus due to a mistaken concern for pulmonary edema. With ongoing massive third-spacing the patient becomes more hypotensive, EtCO2 drops, and PEA arrest follows. The infusion has no volume to act on.
JUDGMENT-ERROR BRANCH #2. The crew correctly repeats IM epi and starts the epi drip but caps fluid at a single 250 mL bolus because 'his lungs are wheezy' (misinterpreting bronchospasm as pulmonary edema). With profound vasodilation and capillary leak the tank stays empty and the patient arrests in PEA. The drip is running into a hose with no water in it. Recovery requires recognition that anaphylactic wheeze is NOT pulmonary edema, opening a second IV/IO, and giving 2-3 L wide open during the resus.

Trigger: Crew pushed atropine 1 mg IV reflexively on the basis of a slow heart rate alone, without first confirming hemodynamic instability and without recognizing the beta-blocker etiology. The protocol's 'least invasive manner possible' and 'consider potential culprit medications' guidance was bypassed.
Crew skipped the perfusion check, saw HR 44 on the monitor, and pushed atropine 1 mg IV before identifying the beta-blocker etiology. Per the protocol's educational pearls, atropine 'may have little or no effect' in beta-blocker–mediated bradycardia. Heart rate bumps transiently from 44 to 50 and drifts back to 46 within 60 seconds. BP is unchanged. The crew is now considering escalation to a second atropine or pacing on an awake, conversational patient with adequate perfusion — neither indicated. The teaching point is that atropine in BB-mediated brady on a compensated patient burns the drug, may not work, and can prompt inappropriate further escalation.

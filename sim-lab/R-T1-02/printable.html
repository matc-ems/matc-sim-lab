Improper Care Branch
Anchored on CHF; NTG + CPAP given
Trigger: Crew skipped multi-field auscultation and JVD/edema assessment, anchored on the chart history of CHF, and initiated CPAP + sublingual nitroglycerin without confirming a fluid-overload picture. Bronchodilators and steroids were not yet given.
Crew anchored on the 'history of CHF' on her med list and the elderly-female-with-SOB picture. Without first auscultating in multiple fields, checking JVD/edema, or trending the onset, they assumed acute pulmonary edema and started: CPAP at 10 cmH2O AND sublingual nitroglycerin 0.4 mg. Bronchodilators and steroids were not given. Two problems are now apparent: (1) the treatment doesn't help, because the underlying physiology is bronchospasm with mucus plugging from a viral URI, not fluid overload; (2) the nitroglycerin drops her BP — she was running 148/86 with COPD-related sympathetic tone, and post-NTG she is 96/58 with new lightheadedness. CPAP is tolerable for her but expiratory air-trapping risks worsening her dynamic hyperinflation. The patient is now actively deteriorating from inappropriate therapy. The teaching point: anchoring on chart history without doing the physical exam can not only fail to treat the real problem — it can introduce iatrogenic harm.

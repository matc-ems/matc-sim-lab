The crew must resist the reflex to push naloxone first and instead manage airway, breathing, circulation, and start working through the competing toxidromes individually.

↳ MATC: Opioid Poisoning/Overdose
critical
Suction prn, open airway, insert OPA
Open and maintain patent airway. If needed, provide head tilt/chin lift, or jaw thrust if concern for potential spinal injury. Suction airway: for significantly contaminated airways, consider utilizing a suction assisted laryngeal airway decontamination (SALAD) technique. Oropharyngeal airways (OPA) or nasopharyngeal airways (NPA) can be placed if needed to maintain a patent airway and make BVM ventilation more effective. OPA are used for patients without gag reflex.
↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical
Two-person BVM with PEEP 5 cmH2O at 10–12 breaths/min, titrate to SpO2 94–98%
Use bag-valve-mask (BVM) ventilation in the setting of respiratory failure or arrest. Whenever possible, the patient's head should be elevated up to 30 degrees. Two-person, two-thumbs-up BVM ventilation is preferred. PEEP should be used with BVM. 5 cmH2O is generally an appropriate initial PEEP setting. Rate — Adult: 10–12 breaths/minute. Ventilate with just enough volume to see chest rise, approximately 6–8 mL/kg ideal body weight.
↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical

Attach cardiac monitor and examine rhythm strip for arrhythmias (consider 12-lead EKG). Monitor pulse oximetry and end-tidal capnography (EtCO2) for respiratory decompensation. Cocaine has sodium channel blocking effects and can cause significant cardiac conduction abnormalities with a widened QRS. Treatment is with sodium bicarbonate similar to a tricyclic antidepressant. Check a 12-lead EKG to assess for these complications.

↳ MATC: Shock
critical
Recognize mixed toxidrome: opioids + stimulant + benzo (not pure opioid)
Toxidromes are constellations of signs and symptoms that aid in the identification of certain classes of medications and their toxic manifestations. These toxidrome constellations may be masked or obscured in polypharmacy events due to counteracting effects of the toxins. Opioids — Respiratory depression, Miosis (pinpoint pupils), Altered mental status, Decreased bowel sounds. Stimulants (Sympathomimetic) — Tachycardia, tachydysrhythmias; Hypertension; Diaphoresis; Delusions/paranoia; Seizures; Hyperthermia; Mydriasis (dilated pupils).

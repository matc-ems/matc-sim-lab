critical
Rapid primary survey (ABC); recognize compromised airway with active aspiration
Implement emergent interventions and monitoring. Physical Examination – Assess for: Abnormal respiratory pattern, rate and/or effort; Quality of air exchange, including depth of respiration and equality of breath sounds; Abnormal breath sounds (e.g., wheezing, rhonchi, rales, or stridor); Skin color (cyanosis or pallor), presence of diaphoresis; Mental status, including anxiety; Airway obstruction with foreign body or swelling.

Oropharyngeal airways (OPA) or nasopharyngeal airways (NPA) can be placed if needed to maintain a patent airway and make BVM ventilation more effective. OPA are used for patients without gag reflex. NPA are used for patients with gag reflex.
↳ MATC: Airway Management (Includes RSI)
critical
Two-person BVM, 10/min, PEEP valve at 5 cmH2O, head elevated 30°
Use bag-valve-mask (BVM) ventilation in the setting of respiratory failure or arrest. Whenever possible, the patient's head should be elevated up to 30 degrees. Two-person, two-thumbs-up BVM ventilation is preferred. PEEP should be used with BVM. 5 cmH2O is generally an appropriate initial PEEP setting. Rate Adult: 10–12 breaths/minute.

Crew arrives to find a 67-year-old male slumped at his kitchen table. He is confused, opens eyes only to voice, mumbles unintelligibly with slurred speech, and has an apparent right-sided facial droop. Skin is pale, cool, and diaphoretic. The presentation looks like a stroke at first glance, but the diaphoresis, tachycardia, and missed-meal-after-insulin history are subtle clues to hypoglycemia. A blood glucose check is the lynchpin assessment.

important

Glucagon IM/IN – an option for patients for whom IV access cannot be established. Adult dosing: 1 mg IM/IN (or prefilled 3 mg dry powder IN or prefilled IM autoinjector).

↳ MATC: Hypoglycemia
Treated for hypoglycemia
Trigger: Patient received D10 25 g IV with appropriate reassessment. Mental status improved from GCS 11 to GCS 15 within 2-3 minutes; the right-sided 'stroke-like' deficits resolved entirely as glucose normalized — the classic fingerprint of a hypoglycemic stroke mimic.
After D10 25 g IV, the patient's mental status clears noticeably within 2-3 minutes. The right facial droop and slurred speech resolve completely, confirming that the deficits were due to neuroglycopenia rather than stroke. Repeat glucose is 112 mg/dL. Crew transports for ED evaluation per protocol — recurrent hypoglycemia is a real risk given his long-acting insulin onboard.

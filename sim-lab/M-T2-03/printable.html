Initial Contact
Crew arrives 4 minutes after dispatch to find a 38-year-old male supine on the office floor in a post-ictal state. Coworker reports the seizure was generalized tonic-clonic, lasted about 3 minutes, and stopped just as EMS pulled up. Patient is mumbling, opens eyes to voice, GCS 11 and slowly trending up. Vitals are stable. While the crew is mid-assessment — taking baseline vitals, applying SpO2, asking about meds — the patient suddenly stiffens and begins another generalized tonic-clonic seizure. This is now a recurrent seizure without recovery between, and as it continues past 2 minutes of unrelenting activity the crew is in status epilepticus territory per protocol definition. The decision-dense window opens here: airway protection, oxygenation, IV/IO vs IM/IN benzo, and the dose math all need to happen simultaneously while the patient is convulsing.

Trigger: Crew misidentified stridor as a choking foreign body and attempted back blows / abdominal thrusts on an awake patient with no history of aspiration. The intervention worsens the patient's distress, increases soft-tissue edema, and pushes the partial obstruction toward complete.
Crew anchored on 'stridor must be a choking foreign body.' Despite the patient being awake, alert, talking, and giving a clear history of NO aspiration (she did not choke, never coughed, food went down fine), the crew positions her forward and delivers back blows; one provider readies for abdominal thrusts. The patient — already anxious from the sensation of a closing throat — panics. Her crying and gasping increase her work of breathing, the soft-tissue edema worsens, the inspiratory stridor becomes louder and now occurs on every breath, and the obstruction trends toward complete. This is the recovery beat: a thoughtful crew recognizes the maneuver is wrong, STOPS, returns the patient to her position of comfort, reassesses, and gives IM epinephrine. Failure to recover here puts her at imminent risk of complete airway closure.

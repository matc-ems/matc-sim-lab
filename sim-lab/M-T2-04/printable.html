Improper Care Branch
Amiodarone given; cardiotox worsens
Trigger: Crew labeled the rhythm as wide-complex tachycardia and gave amiodarone 150 mg IV without recognizing the TCA toxidrome. The amiodarone's sodium-channel blockade stacks on the TCA's, widening the QRS further and driving the patient toward polymorphic VT and circulatory collapse.
Crew anchored on 'wide-complex tachycardia' and applied the cardiac tachycardia protocol — administered amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 minutes for presumed regular wide-complex tachycardia. Amiodarone is itself a sodium-channel blocker (Vaughan-Williams Class III with Class I activity); stacking it on top of the TCA's sodium-channel blockade catastrophically worsens cardiotoxicity. Within minutes the QRS widens further (138 → 168 ms), BP drops to 64/38, and the rhythm degenerates toward torsades-like polymorphic VT. Sodium bicarbonate must now be given urgently along with full resuscitation.

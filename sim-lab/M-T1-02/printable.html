Improper Care Branch
Stroke alert, no glucose checked
Trigger: Crew did not perform a finger-stick blood glucose at the scene. They activated a stroke alert and began transport based on stroke-scale findings alone, bypassing the protocol's explicit hypoglycemia rule-out step. While the underlying diagnosis here is genuinely stroke, the procedural error reflects unsafe practice that in a different patient would have meant missed hypoglycemia.
Crew anchors on the obvious deficits, calls a stroke alert, and transports without performing a finger-stick blood glucose at the scene. The patient does have a true stroke, but skipping the protocol-mandated hypoglycemia rule-out is a documentation and competency failure. On arrival the stroke team takes the patient straight to CT — but because the prehospital glucose was never recorded, the receiving facility delays activation by 4-6 minutes while their own finger-stick is performed and resulted. In a hypoglycemic-mimic patient this same shortcut would have meant a stroke bay was occupied and treatment was withheld. The lesson: the glucose check is non-negotiable on every AMS / stroke patient.

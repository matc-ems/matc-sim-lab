Improper Care Branch

Trigger: Crew gave a single 2.5 mg albuterol nebulizer without ipratropium, based on patient age rather than severity. The protocol's 5 mg dose with ipratropium is indicated for any patient in respiratory distress with bronchospasm signs, including pediatric asthmatics.
Crew assumes a 7-year-old gets a 'pediatric' (read: half) dose and gives albuterol 2.5 mg neb alone, omitting ipratropium. The protocol does not actually carve out a smaller dose for children in respiratory distress with bronchospasm signs — the 2.5 mg dose is for mild wheezing without distress, and this child has clear distress, retractions, and bronchospasm. The treatment helps a little, but inadequately for the severity, and the crew has also omitted the second bronchodilator (ipratropium) that the protocol explicitly pairs with albuterol up to 3 doses. The child plateaus rather than improves. This is a 'lucky outcome' branch — child is not deteriorating, but he is also not getting the protocol-correct treatment for his severity.

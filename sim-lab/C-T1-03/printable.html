Improper Care Branch
NTG given without indication
Trigger: Crew administered nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL (or pursued other aggressive prehospital BP reduction) in response to the BP reading alone, without a neuro exam, ACS findings, or pulmonary edema. Chronic HTN means autoregulation is shifted right; abrupt BP reduction drops cerebral perfusion below the lower limit.
Crew sees 218/126 and thinks 'hypertensive emergency.' Without completing a neuro exam, without an indication of ACS or pulmonary edema, they administer nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL to try to bring the pressure down. Five minutes later, BP has dropped abruptly to 142/78 — a 76-point systolic drop. The patient, whose cerebral autoregulation has been chronically reset upward by years of untreated hypertension, can no longer perfuse his watershed zones. He becomes pale, lightheaded, and develops new slurred speech and a subtle right-sided drift — an iatrogenic watershed stroke. The crew has converted urgency into an emergency they caused.

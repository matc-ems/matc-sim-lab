Improper Care Branch
Adenosine without proper flush
Trigger: Adenosine 6 mg given without immediate rapid 10 mL saline flush and/or without raising the arm. Half-life <10 seconds means the drug never reached the AV node. No asystolic pause on the strip — diagnostic clue that the drug did not reach the heart.
Adenosine 6 mg was pushed but the saline flush was slow, the stopcock wasn't preloaded, or the arm wasn't elevated. The drug was metabolized in the peripheral veins before reaching the AV node. No conversion. Rhythm remains regular narrow-complex at ~192. Patient experienced no 'flush' sensation — a tell that the dose didn't make it centrally. The trap is concluding 'refractory SVT' and escalating prematurely. The correct response is to recognize the technique failure, regroup with a proper setup, and give 12 mg with rapid flush and elevation.

Improper Care Branch
Treated as STABLE — amiodarone given instead
Trigger: Crew misclassified the patient as stable and started amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 minutes; during slow infusion the rhythm deteriorates and the patient briefly loses consciousness — unstable VT now degenerating.
Crew anchored on the wide-complex morphology and started amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 minutes — a stable-VT treatment — without acknowledging the unstable signs (BP 76/46, AMS, chest pain). Three minutes into the infusion the rhythm degenerates from monomorphic VT into polymorphic VT, BP drops further, and the patient briefly loses consciousness. The teaching beat is that protocol explicitly defines unstable wide-complex VT as a cardioversion case, not an amiodarone case. The crew now must abandon the antiarrhythmic, deliver synchronized shock with what sedation they can give safely, and prepare for possible defibrillation if the rhythm becomes pulseless.

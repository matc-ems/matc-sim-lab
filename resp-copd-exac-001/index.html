CRITICAL Immediately recognize pulseless arrest (PEA), announce cardiac arrest, and begin high-quality CPR at 100-120 compressions/min with depth 2-2.4 inches, full recoil
CRITICAL Remove CPAP, insert OPA, begin BVM ventilation at 10 breaths/min (asynchronous with compressions once advanced airway in place) with 15 L/min O2 — maximal oxygen supplementation during CPR
CRITICAL Aggressively address reversible causes — Hypoxia (BVM with O2, advanced airway), Hypercarbia/H+ acidosis (adequate ventilation), Hypovolemia (IV fluid 500 mL NS bolus)
CRITICAL Administer Epinephrine 1 mg (10 mL of 1:10,000 / 0.1 mg/mL) IV/IO every 3-5 minutes during arrest
IMPORTANT After scenario, debrief extensively on the failure point — recognition of NIV failure (rising EtCO2, worsening mental status, paradoxical RR drop) and the need to escalate to BVM with PEEP and RSI before respiratory arrest
Learning Objectives
  • Recognize severe COPD exacerbation and impending respiratory failure from history, physical findings, and waveform capnography (shark fin)
  • Escalate airway and ventilatory support in a protocol-driven stepwise fashion: NC → NRB → nebulized bronchodilators → CPAP → BVM with PEEP → RSI
  • Identify objective signs of NIV failure (rising EtCO2, declining mental status, paradoxical slowing of respiratory rate) and act decisively
  • Safely perform RSI in a bronchospastic patient: preoxygenation, premedication, induction (ketamine preferred for bronchodilation), paralysis, and confirmation with waveform capnography
  • Manage the intubated bronchospastic patient with lung-protective ventilation, prolonged expiratory time, in-line bronchodilators, and adequate post-intubation sedation

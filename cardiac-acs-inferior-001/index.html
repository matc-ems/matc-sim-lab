Learning Objectives
  • Apply the FONA bundle (Fentanyl, Oxygen, Nitroglycerin, Aspirin) as a cognitive checklist for ACS
  • Acquire a 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of patient contact - the primary diagnostic tool for STEMI
  • Identify Inferior STEMI on 12-lead (ST elevation II, III, aVF with reciprocal depression I, aVL)
  • Always acquire V4R in inferior STEMI to evaluate for RV involvement; >1 mm STE in V4R = RV infarct = nitrate caution
  • Verbalize the absolute contraindication to nitroglycerin within 48 hours of phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors
  • Apply the Aspirin loading rule: 324 mg PO chewed even if the patient takes daily 81 mg - the dose is what matters
  • Choose Fentanyl over Morphine in unstable angina/NSTEMI given morphine's mortality association
  • Activate the regional STEMI system from the field and transmit the 12-lead ECG
  • Recognize that inferior STEMI commonly produces bradyarrhythmias and AV blocks - have atropine and pacer pads ready
  • Differentiate between PDE5-induced hypotension and RV-STEMI-induced hypotension, both of which respond to fluids; the former resolves over 30 minutes as NTG is metabolized

Branch: Hypoxic Cardiac Arrest from Delayed RSI
Failure to proceed with RSI within 5 minutes of identifying refractory status
Prolonged preoxygenation without decisive action to secure the airway, combined with ineffective BVM and ongoing metabolic demand from status epilepticus, leads to progressive hypoxia and bradycardic arrest. This branch illustrates the consequence of analysis paralysis in a patient who cannot protect their airway.

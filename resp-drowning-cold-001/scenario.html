Expected Actions
CRITICAL Use ABC approach (airway → breathing → circulation), not CAB — drowning is a hypoxic insult that requires prompt oxygenation and ventilation
CRITICAL Suction airway of water and secretions; do NOT attempt to expel water with abdominal thrusts or positioning — risks aspiration
CRITICAL Begin 2-person BVM with PEEP 5 cmH2O and 100% oxygen to correct profound hypoxemia; support spontaneous respirations
IMPORTANT Mechanism was jumping (not diving), so c-spine injury is less likely; assess for any posterior tenderness or deformity and use clinical judgment — provide manual stabilization if uncertain
CRITICAL Remove wet clothing, dry the patient, and apply warm blankets to limit ongoing heat loss
CRITICAL Obtain core temperature (rectal if available) to guide hypothermia management
CRITICAL Apply cardiac monitor — hypothermic myocardium is prone to dysrhythmia with rough handling
CRITICAL Move the patient gently — hypothermic myocardium can be precipitated into VF by jostling
